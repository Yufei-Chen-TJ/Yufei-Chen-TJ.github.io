<p>
  <strong>Yufei Chen</strong>, Ph.D., is an <strong>Associate Professor</strong> and Doctoral Supervisor at the School of Computer Science and Technology, <strong>Tongji University</strong>. She previously served as a guest researcher at the Fraunhofer Institute for Computer Graphics Research (Fraunhofer IGD), Germany.
</p>

<p>
  Her main research interests lie in <strong>machine learning</strong> and <strong>medical data analysis</strong>, with a focus on multi-source medical image fusion and decision-making risk in intelligent medical diagnosis systems. She has proposed multiple safe and trustworthy deep learning methods and translated them into real-world clinical applications. She has published <strong>100+ papers</strong> in top-tier journals and conferences, including TMI, TNNLS, TCSVT, MIA, NeurIPS, CVPR, ICLR, AAAI, ACM MM, MICCAI, and BIBM, among others. She holds <strong>19 granted invention patents</strong>, and has contributed as a core member to the development of <strong>three group standards</strong>.
</p>

<p>
  In recent years, she has led more than <strong>20 research projects</strong>, including projects funded by the National Key R&D Program of China, the National Natural Science Foundation of China, and the Key Project of the Shanghai Municipal Education Commission, among others. As a principal contributor, she has received multiple honors, including the <strong>First Prize</strong> of Shanghai Technical Invention Award, the <strong>Third Prize</strong> of Shanghai Science and Technology Progress Award, the <strong>Third Prize</strong> of Zhejiang Provincial Science and Technology Progress Award, and the <strong>First Prize</strong> of Ningbo Science and Technology Progress Award. She has also won the <strong>Second Prize</strong> in the National Teaching Innovation Competition of China, and the <strong>Special Prize</strong> in the Teaching Innovation Competition of Shanghai. Over the past five years, she has supervised students to win <strong>18 awards</strong> at international, national, and provincial-level competitions.
</p>

<p>
  In addition, she serves as <strong>Chair</strong> of MICCAI-CLIP, <strong>Education Chair</strong> of MICCAI-SIG-xMedIA, Committee Member of the National Technical Committee on Biomimetics Standardization, Vice Chair of MICS Webinar, and Executive Committee Member of the CCF Technical Committee on Computer Applications.
</p>
